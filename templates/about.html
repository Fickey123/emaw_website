About Us

Our Background

The Evangelical Missions Across the World is a registered church founded on Christian values. The church was registered in 2009 in Kenya. The founder of EMAW church is Bishop Daniel Muli.

The founder observed the great need of the people and believed that the church should provide solutions spiritually, socially, economically and mentally.

Since the beginning, EMAW has actively been involved in evangelism, discipleship, planting new churches, ordaining ministers, organizing conferences, and assisting the oppressed.

As a church, we are called to serve the people of God with the authorized and inspired word of God to bring transformation in the lives of people. (Romans 12:2)

Vision
To advance God's kingdom through Evangelism and Discipleship.
Mission
To fulfill the great commission by making disciples through teaching the word of God and equiping of believers.
Focus
To grow together as each part of the body of Christ does its work.

Our Objectives

  • a. To preach and spread the word of God.
  • b. To build, buy or hire and maintain the church building, social halls e.t.c.
  • c. To baptize, conduct weddings, burials, conferences and community social work as to improve living standards of the people.
  • d. To assist the needy like orphans, widows and street children.
  • e. To plant churches, to ordain and license pastors to carry out the objectives of the EMAW church.
  • f. To organize christian rallies, conventions, camps, street meetings and open air crusades in church halls, tents and other convenient places.
  • g. To enter into lease agreements with individuals, government for hire of buildings, houses, open space to enhance the objectives of EMAW church
  • h. To ask and accept donations from members, government, charitable organizations, non-members and any person corporate or private from all parts of the world.
  • i. To borrow money from banks, Saccos, charitable organizations or well wishers as recommended by the executive board committee. The monies borrowed must be used exclussively for enhancing the objectives of EMAW church.
  • j. The organization of EMAW is non political and non profit making.
  • k. To buy goods and services necessary for the furtherance of objectivesof the EMAW church, including building materials, training equipments and stationeries.

Our Statement of Faith

  • EMAW church believes in the fundamental teaching of the bible, Gods infallible written word "the sixty-six books of old and new testament".
  • a) We believe there is one true God, externally existing in three persons - father, son and THe Holy Spirit -(holy trinity)- that is three in one and one in three. Deutronomy 6:4, Mathew 28:18, John 1:1-3, 18, Acts 5:3-4
  • b) We believe the bible to be inspired authoritative word of God. II Peter 1:21.
  • c) We believe in the deity of Jesus Christ, his incarmation, crucification, define bodily resurrection, his ascension to glory and his promisedsecond coming. 1 Cor 15:4,14,20, Luke 24:36-43, John 20:24-29, Acts 1:9-11, Ephesians 1:20, 1 Timothy 2:5, Mathew 24:44, Rev. 19:1-22.
  • d) We believe in Jesus power to save, to heal, to sustain in christians to live holy and righteous life.
  • e) We believe in salvation through the redeeming blood of Jesus Christ, Hebrews 9:22.
  • f) We believe in water baptism to the saved, public holy matrimony, dedication of children and the lords supper as commanded in the Holy Bible, Mark 16:16, Mark 10:7, 1 Samuel 1:20, 1 Cor. 11:28
  • g) We believe in the bodily resurrection, eternal life for the righteous and eternal punishment for the wicked. 2 Cor. 5:8, Phillipians 1:23, Luke 23:39-43, 1 Cor. 15:12-58, 1 Thessolonians 4:13-18.
  • h) We believe Jesus Christ is the head of the church, his body which is composed of all men, living and dead who have been joined to him through saving faith, Collosians 1:18, Ephesians 1:22.
  • i) We believe Jesus Christ commanded all believers to proclaim the gospel throughout the world and to disciple men of every nation, Mthew 28:18-20.
  • j) We believe in the operation of the gifts of the Holy Spirit as enumerated in (1 Corinthians 12:12,-14, Romans 12:6-8) as manifested in the early church.
